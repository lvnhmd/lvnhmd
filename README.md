I'm a Senior Software Engineer with 18 years building production systems in TypeScript, Node.js, and AWS — currently at Times Higher Education, previously at the BBC, Samsung, FIFA's official World Cup app, Nando's, and the UK Ministry of Justice.

Outside of full-time work, I'm building two things:

### 🤖 Marvin — a Multi-Agent Credit Decision System
[lvnhmd/challenger](https://github.com/lvnhmd/challenger) — Marvin is a multi-agent system for credit decisioning. Three specialised LLM agents — an **Approver**, a **Rejector**, and a **Judge** — run a structured debate over each application and produce a credit decision with a full reasoning trail: the case for approval, the case against, the debate exchange, and the Judge's verdict. A guardrail layer surfaces hard regulatory rules transparently when they override the LLM's conclusion, and every session is persisted as an immutable audit record in PostgreSQL.

The point is to fix what black-box credit models break: explainability by design, adversarial rigour, and an audit trail that holds up to regulatory review. This is where most of my AI engineering effort goes — agent orchestration, prompt design, structured output, streaming, evaluation against real loan datasets, and the un-glamorous work of making an AI system give answers that hold up in a domain where being wrong has real financial consequences.

### 📚 Money OS — a platform and course for beginners
A simple, structured way to understand, manage, and start investing your money. Built for people with income but low clarity — especially women and first-time investors who feel locked out.
